LRP-IntDB Help & Tutorial

LRP-IntDB (LRP-1 Interactome Database) is a manually curated, cell-type-specific protein–protein interaction resource exclusively dedicated to the Low-density Lipoprotein Receptor-related Protein 1 (LRP-1). This tutorial will walk you through all the features of the web interface, from searching for ligands to downloading custom datasets.

1 Home Page – Search Options

The Home page provides two complementary ways to access the data:

A) Search by protein (ligand)

Use this option when you already know a specific LRP-1 ligand (protein, gene, or Uniprot ID) and want to know whether its interaction with LRP‑1 has been validated, in which cell types, and with what confidence.

How to use it:

  1. Locate the search box on the upper part of the Home page.
  2. Enter any of the following identifiers:
    • Protein name (e.g., “Apolipoprotein E”, “APOE”)
    • Gene name (e.g., “APOE”, “PRNP”)
    • UniProt ID (e.g., “P02649”)
  3. Click Search or press Enter.

What you will see:

  • A summary card with the ligand’s:
    • Full protein name, gene name, and UniProt accession.
    • Subcellular location (e.g., secreted, membrane, cytoplasmic).
    • Function – a brief description of the protein’s biological role.
  • A table listing all cell types or tissues where the ligand–LRP‑1 interaction has been experimentally verified.
  • Each interaction is assigned a confidence score (see Score Interpretation below).

B) Search by cell type / tissue

Use this option to explore the full repertoire of LRP‑1 ligands known to interact in a specific cellular context. This is particularly useful for hypothesis generation in disease‑ or tissue‑specific research.

How to use it:

  1. On the Home page, locate the dropdown menu labelled “Select cell type / tissue”.
  2. Choose one of the 26 available cell types/tissues (e.g., neuron, hepatocyte, macrophage, brain, liver, etc.).
  3. Alternatively, select “Others” to view ligands that have been experimentally validated but not yet assigned to a specific cell type.

What you will see:

  • A complete list of ligands that interact with LRP‑1 in the chosen cell type.
  • For each ligand, the database displays:
    • Protein name, gene name, and UniProt ID.
    • Specific cell line or tissue used in the original study (e.g., “SH‑SY5Y”, “mouse brain lysate”).
    • Confidence score (see below).
  • Interactive network visualization – generated dynamically with Cytoscape.js – showing all ligands from your selection, color‑coded by their validation level (e.g., high/medium/low confidence). You can zoom, drag, and hover over nodes to see details.

2 Understanding the Confidence Score

Each interaction in LRP-IntDB is scored based on the strength and reproducibility of experimental evidence:

0.8 – 1.0
High confidence
validated by multiple orthogonal methods or in multiple independent studies.
0.5 – 0.79
Medium confidence
supported by at least one well‑controlled study, but not yet widely replicated.
0.1 – 0.49
Low confidence
preliminary evidence, single report, or non‑standard assay.
Note: Scores are intended as a guide for prioritization; even low‑scoring interactions may be biologically relevant in specific contexts.

6 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Do I need to register or log in to use LRP-IntDB?
A: No. The database is completely open and does not require registration.
Q: How often is LRP-IntDB updated?
A: We continuously monitor the literature. Major updates are released annually; minor corrections are incorporated as they are identified.
Q: Can I submit a newly discovered LRP-1 interaction?
A: Yes! Please send the details (ligand, cell type, experimental method, PubMed ID) to the contact emails above. We will review and include it in the next update.
Q: What does “Others” mean in the cell type dropdown?
A: It contains ligands that have been experimentally validated but the original publication did not specify a clear cell type or tissue (e.g., studies using purified proteins or cell‑free systems). We keep them as “unassigned” until further evidence becomes available.
Q: Are predicted interactions included?
A: No. LRP-IntDB only includes experimentally validated interactions. We do not incorporate computational predictions or orthology transfers.
