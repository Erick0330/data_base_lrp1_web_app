About LRP-IntDB

LRP-IntDB (LRP-1 Interactome Database) is a manually curated, pharmacologically oriented protein-protein interaction resource exclusively dedicated to the Low-density Lipoprotein Receptor-related Protein 1 (LRP-1). Developed by the Protein Modelling and Crystallography Group at the Center for Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology (CIGB, Cuba), LRP-IntDB was born from a concrete limitation: mainstream PPI databases capture less than 15% of the experimentally validated interactions of LRP-1 and provide little information on their tissue or cellular context.

Unlike general-purpose interactome resources, LRP-IntDB offers:

High-confidence, literature-curated interactions following PRISMA-based methodology.
Tissue and cell-type specificity, enabling researchers to distinguish context-relevant interactions.
Functional enrichment analysis linked to diseases where LRP-1 is a therapeutic target (neurodegeneration, cancer, atherosclerosis, viral infection).

LRP-IntDB is freely available and committed to open science. We believe that precision in target characterization is the first step toward precision therapeutics.
